HOW TO PLAY

Guess the CUBLE in as few tries as you can.

Each guess must be a solvable cube. Status indicators for corner/edge orientation and permutation parity are provided for your convenience.

Notes and Tips

To help get you started, the results of guessing the solved cube are shown first. This free guess does not count against your score.

You can only use each piece once, so you will first have to erase one piece to change the color of another.

To ensure the cube always stays in a solvable state, try to limit yourself to the following actions:

  • Flipping two edges
  • Twisting three corners once
  • Twisting one corner twice and two corners once
  • Cycling three pieces while preserving their orientations
  • Swapping two PAIRS of pieces while preserving their orientations

Fixing an Unsolvable Cube

If edge parity (EP) is nonzero, flip any edge.

If corner parity (CP) is nonzero, twist one or more corners until it reaches zero.

If permutation parity (PP) is nonzero, swap any two pieces.
